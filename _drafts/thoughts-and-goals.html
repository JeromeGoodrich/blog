It's been a while... and I miss it.

I started feeling mentally constipated if I don't write things down.

Mainly I guess I'm just tired... I get home, and the easiest thing to do is "abrutie" myself in front of the screen. It's become a bad habit. It seems automatic and compulsory, and even when I realize I'm doing it, I still seem to not be able to stop.

I told myself, that tonight I would write down some of the things I could be doing instead.

  • Learning Spanish
  • Reading a Book
  • Working on a side project
  • Writing a blog
  • Going for a walk
  • Cleaning up

This is definitely a common refrain, and yet every time I'm aware enough to change what I'd deem suboptimal behavior, I'm always hopeful that things will change. In the past I've always painted youtube and it's various substitutes as things to avoid, and while I still agree with that, I think that I'm now more interested in being interested in other things. I think it's about time to have goals again. Things to look forward to and shoot for. Maybe even start making some small lists again. Each of these things listed above I care about. I want to learn Spanish because I love Sol and want to be able to be more integrated an in tune with the important people in her life, and as a bonus it's a great skill to have.

I've just been struck with the thought, goals are great but I have tendency to make them to intense and work-like. I think  what I'm more interested in now are projects.

  • I'd like to complete duolingo in spanish by our trip to Portugal
  • The reading thing is hard. I think that ought to just be th new default. If i'm not playing around with a project, I think it makes sense to be reading. It's also fertilizer for writing
  • writing is also important. I'd like to get back to where I'm doing a blog about whatever 4 time's a week.
  • get the fitbit back on a and keep it charged aim for 15,000 steps a day

I think my homework is figuring what kind of small project and in what domain I'd like to explore. I think waza provides a nice venue for machine learning  stuff, but I still need to pick a project. In addition to that maybe I can come up with a list of some good introductory DIY projects in some adjacencies.

I think I'm getting close to be able to start doing some low-level physical stuff as well. It would be nice to get back into the early morning routine.

So today, I resisted the urge to watch youtube while I ate, and ended up working on being able to access the ebay_clone repo from my machine. pretty happy with how I spent the time. Considering I figured it out and learned some things in the process.
